What is a Regex?

A regular expression (regex) is a sequence of characters that defines a search pattern. It is often used for string matching and manipulation. Regexes are powerful tools for text validation, extraction, and replacing.

How unique is a Regex?

Regex patterns are unique depending on the characters and sequences you define. They allow you to match specific string patterns like email addresses, phone numbers, or even specific words or characters.

How are Regexes used?

Regular expressions are commonly used in programming for input validation, text searching and replacing, and text parsing. For example, they are often used to validate email addresses, postal codes, or phone numbers.
